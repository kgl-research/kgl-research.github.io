KGL Research Lab

The Co-Design (Cognition and Computation in Design) Lab is a multidisciplinary research group at UC Berkeley housed primarily in the Department of Mechanical Engineering and affiliated with the Jacobs Institute for Design Innovation. We are part of the Berkeley Institute of Design (BiD); our office/lab is located within BiD in the Hearst Memorial Mining Building, and we enjoy close collaboration with this network of colleagues spanning expertise in human-computer interaction, information science, education, architecture, and art practice.

The Co-Design Lab conducts scholarship at the frontiers of design research. We are interested in understanding the mechanisms that allow both humans and computers to excel at design tasks, and leveraging this information to develop the next generation of methods and tools that fundamentally improve the design process. Methodologically, our research group conducts controlled experiments utilizing both behavioral and computational methods (e.g., neuroimaging studies of design thinking), systems research (e.g., interactive prototypes and tools for designers), as well as in-situ studies of student and professional designers (e.g., design method usage in engineering education).

Some of our highest activity areas are encapsulated by the following keywords:

Design theory, methodology, and automation; design cognition; neuroimaging methods applied to design; human-machine collaboration; decision-making applied to engineering teams and individuals; ideation and creativity; analogical reasoning in design; preference modeling and design attribute optimization; sustainable design; new product development; crowdsourcing

You can learn more about our work by looking at our research papers. To view current Lab members and Alumni please visit the Team page.
